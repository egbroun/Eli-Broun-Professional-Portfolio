Location Quotient Analysis Map

This map visualizes an LQ analysis for NC Counties. The Location Quotient can be defined as the share of employment in a given industry in a specified subarea as a percentage of the share of employment in the same industry within the larger area. In this context, it means which counties had their percentage of workers in furniture manufacturing above or below the statewide percentage of workers in furniture. A LQ value greater than 100 means they had above the state average .

×

RMI Analysis

To explore the correlation between crime rate and immigration in Texas, I utilized Rank Mean Index or RMI which represents how a subarea has moved in rank for a variable compared to others in the larger area over a time period. I compared county's RMI for crime and immigration between the years 2010 and 2022. If there was a correlation between the variables we would see simialr RMI's for both variables in a county. Using the equation (R2​+R1​)/(R2​−R1​) we see that there appears to be no correlation between crime and immigration in Texas from 2010 and 2022.
